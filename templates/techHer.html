Inspiring the next generation of Girls Who Code

TechHer Conference

TechHer is a one-day immersive technology outreach experience designed to inspire the generation of Girls Who Code through engineering games, mock classes, and exposure to real college pathways.

Event Highlights View Gallery

Event Overview

What TechHer was all about

TechHer was created to introduce high school girls to the possibilities within computer science and engineering through a full day of interactive experiences. Students had the opportunity to connect with college mentors, participate in hands-on activities, hear from faculty and professionals, and explore what a future in tech can look like.

A full day of inspiration

Here’s a simple recap of how the day flowed.

Opening Ceremony

The day began with a warm welcome from CECS Iterim Dean Zakarian , opening remarks from GWC advisors Elizabeth Edenstrom and Sjritia Das, an introduction to the goals of TechHer from GWC President Alyamama Abdo, and a presentation from Admissions on becoming a Wolverine.

Mock Classes Sessions

Students rotated through mock classes exploring AI and engineering principles led by UM-Dearborn Professor Song, Das, and Kasravi.

Scavenger Hunt

A campus-wide interactive challenge designed to build teamwork and exploration of engineering and computer science topics. With cool chances to win iPads, headphones, and gift cards for winning teams.

Celebration & Closing

Students wrapped up the event with reflections, recognition for winning teams, announced scholarship winner, and reminding the girls they are meant to be here!

Why TechHer matters

TechHer is about more than a single event. It is about helping young girls see that they belong in spaces of innovation, leadership, and technology. By creating these experiences, we hope to continue inspiring confidence, curiosity, and long-term impact for the next generation of Girls Who Code.
