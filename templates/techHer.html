Event Overview

What TechHer was all about

TechHer was created to introduce high school girls to the possibilities within computer science and engineering through a full day of interactive experiences. Students had the opportunity to connect with college mentors, participate in hands-on activities, hear from faculty and professionals, and explore what a future in tech can look like.

Highlights

What the girls experienced throughout the day

Opening Session

The day began with a warm welcome, opening remarks, and an introduction to the goals of TechHer. Students were invited into a space centered on confidence, exploration, and possibility.

Hands-On Activities

Students participated in engaging engineering and technology activities that gave them a chance to build, problem-solve, and see themselves reflected in hands-on learning.

Mock Classes

Faculty-led sessions introduced students to topics within engineering and computer science, giving them a glimpse into what learning in these fields can look like at the college level.

Mentorship & Connection

Throughout the event, students connected with college mentors, volunteers, and speakers who encouraged them, answered questions, and shared their own journeys in STEM.

Scavenger Hunt & Exploration

Interactive challenges kept students engaged while encouraging them to explore the space, collaborate with one another, and make the most of every part of the day.

Closing Celebration

The event ended with a celebration of everything the students had experienced, recognizing participation, sharing encouragement, and ending the day on an uplifting note.

Why TechHer matters

TechHer is about more than a single event. It is about helping young girls see that they belong in spaces of innovation, leadership, and technology. By creating these experiences, we hope to continue inspiring confidence, curiosity, and long-term impact for the next generation of Girls Who Code.
