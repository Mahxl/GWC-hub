About Girls Who Code UM-Dearborn

Built on sisterhood, leadership, and impact

Girls Who Code at the University of Michigan-Dearborn is a student-led chapter committed to closing the gender gap in technology by building spaces where students feel empowered to learn, lead, and make an impact in their communities.

Our Mission

Our mission is to empower women in technology through community, technical learning, professional development, and outreach. We want every student who joins our chapter to feel supported in growing their confidence, skills, and sense of belonging.

We are not just preparing students for careers in tech. We are building leaders, mentors, advocates, and changemakers who will shape the future of the field.

The people behind the mission

Our board and interns help bring every workshop, mentorship opportunity, and community event to life.

Board Members

The board leads chapter strategy, programming, outreach, partnerships, and member experience.

Interns

Our interns grow through leadership development, hands-on planning experience, and mentorship from the board.

Moments that matter

From workshops and networking events to outreach and large-scale initiatives, our chapter is rooted in meaningful experiences.

What we do

Our work is centered around creating meaningful, lasting pathways into technology and leadership.

Technical Workshops

Hands-on learning opportunities across multiple areas of tech.

Mentorship

Outreach

Programs and initiatives designed to inspire the next generation.
